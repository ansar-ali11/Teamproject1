Experience the blend of history and modernity in India's vibrant capital.

About Delhi

Delhi, the capital of India, is a city rich in history and culture. With its historic landmarks like the Red Fort and India Gate, the city offers a unique blend of old and new. The vibrant culture is reflected in its festivals, food, and bustling bazaars. Delhi is also a hub for politics, business, and education, often referred to as the heart of India. Enjoy the warmth of its people, the aroma of its street food, and the beauty of its gardens, including the famous Lodhi Gardens and Mughal Gardens.

Must-Visit Places in Delhi

Red Fort

This 17th-century fort is an iconic symbol of Delhi. Explore its stunning architecture and learn about its historical significance.

India Gate

A war memorial dedicated to Indian soldiers who died in World War I. Enjoy a leisurely walk around the lush lawns and fountains.

Qutub Minar

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this towering minaret is a marvel of Indo-Islamic architecture. Don't miss the Qutub Festival held annually.

Humayun's Tomb

The garden tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun, known for its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.

Akshardham Temple

A magnificent temple complex showcasing India's ancient art, culture, and architecture. Enjoy the evening light and sound show.

Lodhi Gardens

A sprawling park with tombs dating back to the Lodi dynasty, offering a serene escape amidst the city's hustle and bustle.
